  • Lightweight Anonymous Authentication and Communication Protocol for Internet of Vehicles

    Funding Agencies: GITA, India and MOST, Taiwan
    Name of Principal Investigator(PI): Dr. Debasis Das

    The present work expects an efficient and secure data transmission scheme to exchange information from a vehicle to other devices (i.e., V2X communication) and vice versa. Besides the security incorporation, this project enhances vehicle communication through different communication technologies, such as DSRC, LTE, 4G/5G, WiMAX, etc. The heterogeneous vehicular network framework of IoV has significant potential to guide and supervise vehicles. It provides a reliable communication platform for abundant mobile Internet and multimedia related applications. The realization of heterogeneous vehicular network architecture is a challenging task. Furthermore, the consumers can enjoy several benefits of the proposed IoV network architecture for onboard computations and communications capable vehicles. The proposed work will be compared with existing technologies in terms of functionalities, such as reliability, user experience, and communication speed. The project is designed for three years. In the first year, a comprehensive analysis will be done to identify various new and existing threats under different security specifications, which enables us to formulate concrete research problems, thereby a robust data exchange strategy. Besides, we will start the architecture design of the proposed work in the second half of this year. In the second year, we will finish all the schemes construction with their formal security analyses. In the third year, we will unite all the cryptographic solutions to develop an application to measure the realization of this work. We believe that this new project will begin a new extent in vehicular communication in the future.

  • Energy Efficient Communication and Data Flow in Smart City using CRN based IoT Framework

    Funding Agencies: Department of Science & Technology (DST), Government of India
     Name of Principal Investigator(PI): Dr. Debasis Das

    In order to ensure the efficiency of the various systems that make up a smart city, it is imperative that there exist protocols that maintain robust connectivity and allow secure and fast information transfer between the IoT devices involved in the communication process. One of the important needs comes from bandwidth allocation to IoT devices. As IoT devices are expected to grow in large numbers, it will be difficult to allocate spectrum bands to these devices. Fixed spectrum assignment policy requires the cost to purchase spectrum therefore spectrum assignment for such a large number of devices can create unnecessary expenditures. Also, the number of licensed users also called Primary Users i.e., PUs will also increase, and the spectrum is a limited resource that will not be able to fulfill the future demands. Since traditional communication techniques do not support spectrum sharing using opportunistic spectrum access, CRNs can be one of the potential solutions and facilities in all of these situations using dynamic spectrum access. Hence, CRN enabled IoT may perceive the spectrum environment and fulfill the spectrum demand of IoT applications. LTE and 5G network has some drawbacks of outreach in the urban area, but with the CRN framework, we can use different frequency to reach the maximum number of devices and users.
